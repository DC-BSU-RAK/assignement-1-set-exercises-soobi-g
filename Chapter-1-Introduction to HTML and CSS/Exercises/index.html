Algerian Mahjeb

Mahjeb is a delicious Algerian street food filled with tomatoes, onions, and spices It's a popular food in Algeria .

Ingredients

  • 2 cups semolina flour
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 cup warm water
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 3 large tomatoes, diced
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • 1 teaspoon cumin
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil (for filling)

Method

  1. Make the dough: In a large bowl, combine semolina flour, all-purpose flour, and salt. also add warm water and olive oil, kneading until you get elastic dough. Cover and leve it for 30 minutes.
  2. make the filling: heat the olive oil in a pan with medium heat. Add onions and garlic, saut√© until soft. Add tomatoes and spices, cook until the mixture thickens (about 10-15 minutes). Set on thesideb to cool.
  3. Shape the mahjeb: Divide the dough into 8 similar balls. On a lightly floured surface, flatten each ball into a thin circle (about 5 inches in diameter).
  4. Add the filling: Place 2 tablespoons of the tomato mixture in the center of each circle. Fold the edges over to enclose the filling, then flatten gently with your hands.
  5. Cook the mahjeb: Heat a skillet or griddle over medium heat. Cook each mahjeb for 3-4 minutes on each side until golden brown spots appear.
  6. Serve: Enjoy warm! Mahjeb is best eaten fresh but can be stored in an airtight container for up to 2 days.
